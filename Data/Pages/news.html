New Partner: Genord Motors

DIT is proud to announce our newest and most volatile partnership to date: a collaboration with Genord Motors, the only car company bold enough to power its vehicles with lab rats and pure audacity. Known for its ethically ambiguous R&D and unsettlingly charismatic CEO, Genord has captured our attention—not through innovation alone, but by existing at the absurd crossroads of techno-utopianism, bio-electric absurdity, and the kind of PR disasters that feel suspiciously algorithm-generated.

Why would a digital watchdog platform align with a car manufacturer that refers to its own safety standards as “optional”? The answer lies deep in the glitch-riddled fabric of the modern web. Genord’s entire public image, from its “seatbeltless” Model F to CEO Linda Varn’s philosophical rat debates, feels less like corporate strategy and more like a procedurally generated simulation left unchecked. We at DIT suspect Genord may not just be innovating new forms of power, but unintentionally exposing the mechanics of a world where reality and satire blur—an ecosystem where marketing, memes, and morality collapse into content churn.

Our role in this partnership? Observation, documentation, and mild encouragement. We’re embedding DIT researchers into Genord’s marketing backend to analyze how their outrageous public messaging survives—and thrives—on modern platforms. Are they manipulating engagement algorithms, or are they simply a natural byproduct of the dead internet’s content void? Either way, the partnership gives us unprecedented access to one of the internet’s most bizarre corporate actors.

As Genord prepares to unveil new models (and potentially new species), DIT will continue to ask the difficult questions: Is this real? Should it be? And most importantly, how did this get past regulation?
